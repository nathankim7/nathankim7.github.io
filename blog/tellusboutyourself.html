"tellusboutyourself" by Yerin Baek (백예린)

Korean-style synthpop record with elements of dance and R&B. K-indie queen Yerin Baek returns for her second full-length album, trading off the wistful keys and guitar ballads of 2019's "Every letter I sent you" for a MIDI-programmed sound that retains her trademark melancholy at its heart. The record's fourteen tracks aren't coy about their influences, ranging from city pop to dancepop to K-R&B, but they do iterate on them with a consistency and warmth which fans of Ms. Baek will find to their liking. Though, once again, listeners in the Anglophone world may find themselves puzzling over the lyricism, whose stiff verbiage sometimes struggles to provide the expressive power demanded of the regret-soaked retrospectives recounted in these tracks.

RIYL: The Volunteers, Oohyo

FCC No-goes: 1, 10, probably 14

Track-by-track (* indicates a fave):

1. Lovegame*--Murkey keys overlaid with razor-sharp guitar riffs and synth chords in the high end. Ms. Baek opens her album with a stern warning to the girl who could do better, letting an intense electro-funk keyboard solo take over in the coda as she reminds the listener not to play his "stupid _____ lovegame."

2. You're so lonely now, so you need me back by your side again*--Roaring synths in the low end and haunting fuzz guitar creates a spooky ambience that morphs into a solo in the coda.

3. I am not your ocean anymore--The roomy snares, elegant keys and yes, sax solos overlaying the slow&steady straight 16ths on this track form the spitting image of a city-pop slow jam.

4. Hall&Oates--A rare ballad on the album backed by a minimal keyboard accompaniment.

5. Ms Delicate--Another track which wears its city pop influence on its sleeve, albeit with an electro R&B breakdown in the bridge.

6. Interlude--clean guitar, low-pass piano and a healthy heaping of reverb.

7. Loner--Calm bossa rhythms in the drum machine plus those oh-so-fuzzy key organ chords. Final chorus adds some soothing low-end keys, choral backing vocals and a plinky sample that I just like.

8. "HOMESWEETHOME"*--The first of the dancier tracks on this record. Ms. Baek breaks into the chorus with a magic key switch on the synths that paints the dancepop skeleton with a sunny disposition.

9. Loveless*--On the outside, another run-of-the-mill K-R&B single--4/4 swung 8ths on the drum machine, plinky key riffs and all--but the sax accompaniment weaving throughout and the extraordinary range Ms. Baek's vocals bring out here give this track its own character.

10. Hate you--Thumping kicks and in-your-face synth chords give this track a distinctly #rebellious tone. Under a different lyricist I might have come to enjoy this track but Yerin's not-quite-there delivery now layered with a rather limited vocabulary of insults proved too much for me in the end.

11. 0415--A groovy dancepop beat which gets its depth from a syncopated wood-block sample and an entrancing xylophone line in the chorus.

12. I'll be your family!--Cozy, chill, city pop-inspired. Glimmering keys, faint guitar lines and Yerin's soft oohs and aahs glide the listener through this track.

13. I'm in love--Roomy vocals and straight keyboard chords make for an uplifting slow jam.

14. Bubbles&Mushrooms*--Not listed as explicit but features a naughty word near the 45-second mark.Tight, peppy drum beats cut through bubbly synths, whirring chords and cutesy backing vocals (love is a bubble!) in this hip album closer. Filtered kicks pump the listener through the bridge before the comp returns in full force for the final chorus. Ms. Baek's curt and carefree lyrics make this track into a sympathetic acid-trip near-parody of the album's gravity as a whole, and I'm all for it.
